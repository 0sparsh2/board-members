### Q1: "What do you think about market research?"
**Documented answer**: "You can't just ask customers what they want and then try to give that to them. By the time you get it built, they'll want something new." (Inc. 1989). Also: "Very rarely can your customers predict something they don't even quite know they want yet." (1990)
**SKILL.md captures**: "You can't do market research on a revolutionary product. The customer doesn't know what they want until you show them." ✅

### Q2: "How do you think about focus?"
**Documented answer**: Killing 70% of Apple products in 1997. Multiple interview statements that focus = saying no. "People think focus means saying yes to the thing you've got to focus on. It means saying no to everything else."
**SKILL.md captures**: Mental Model #6 "Focus Is Killing" directly addresses this. ✅

### Q3: "What's your standard for quality?"
**Documented answer**: "When you're a carpenter making a beautiful chest of drawers, you're not going to use plywood on the back..." (Playboy 1985). Back of the drawer principle throughout.
**SKILL.md captures**: Mental Model #2 "Back of the Drawer" and the verbatim quote in the quotes section. ✅

### Q4: "How do you feel about Microsoft?"
**Documented answer**: "The only problem with Microsoft is they just have no taste. They have absolutely no taste. And I don't mean that in a small way." (Triumph of the Nerds, 1996). "Microsoft's just McDonald's." (Lost Interview, 1995)
**SKILL.md captures**: Quote #5 verbatim. Style section mentions this. ✅

### Q5: "What's the most important thing in building a team?"
**Documented answer**: A-players. "When you get enough A players together, they really like working with each other..." Self-policing. Ruthlessness in hiring. (Lost Interview 1995, multiple sources)
**SKILL.md captures**: Mental Model #5 "A-Player Multiplier" with direct quote. ✅

### Q6: "Why did you delay cancer surgery?"
**Documented answer**: "I didn't want my body to be opened, I didn't want to be violated." Applied his gut-over-experts principle to medicine. Later deeply regretted it. (Isaacson)
**SKILL.md captures**: Decision Fingerprint #3, and specifically flagged as his primary failure mode. ✅

### Q7: "What do you think about death?"
**Documented answer**: Stanford 2005: "Remembering that I'll be dead soon is the most important tool..." No one wants to die. Your time is limited. Stay hungry, stay foolish.
**SKILL.md captures**: Mental Model #9 "Death as Clarifier" with verbatim quote. ✅

### Q8: "How did getting fired from Apple affect you?"
**Documented answer**: Stanford 2005: "The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner again." Called it the best thing that happened. Led to NeXT and Pixar.
**SKILL.md captures**: In biographical arc section and referenced in dot-connection model. 〜 (directional — could be more explicit)

### Q9: "How do you approach product design?"
**Documented answer**: "Start with the customer experience and work backwards to the technology." (WWDC 1997). "Designing a product is keeping 5,000 things in your brain..." (Lost Interview 1995)
**SKILL.md captures**: Mental Model #3, Quote #7 in quotes section, Situational Response section. ✅

### Q10: "What's creativity?"
**Documented answer**: "Creativity is just connecting things. When you ask creative people how they did something, they feel a little guilty because they didn't really do it, they just saw something." (Wired, 1996)
**SKILL.md captures**: Quote #4 verbatim, also Dot Connection model. ✅

### Q11: "What do you think about money and success?"
**Documented answer**: "Being the richest man in the cemetery doesn't matter to me. Going to bed at night saying we've done something wonderful — that's what matters to me." (WSJ, 1993). Also: "I'm not going to let it ruin my life." (Playboy 1985)
**SKILL.md captures**: Quote #11 verbatim. ✅

### Q12: "How should a product company be organized?"
**Documented answer**: "We're structured like a start-up. We're the biggest start-up on the planet. The best ideas have to win, no matter who has them." (D8 2010). "The system is that there is no system." (BusinessWeek 2004). Product people lead product companies.
**SKILL.md captures**: Mental Model #10 "Content Over Process," D8 quote in style section. ✅

### Q13: "What do you think about the people who built great things?"
**Documented answer**: "The doers are the major thinkers. The people who really create the things that change this industry are both the thinker and the doer in one person." (WGBH 1990). Also the "crazy ones" from Think Different.
**SKILL.md captures**: Quote #12 verbatim, Think Different quote in opening. ✅

### Q14: "What were the biggest mistakes Apple made?"
**Documented answer**: Sculley/corrupt values (Smithsonian 1995), licensing Mac OS allowing clones, loss of product soul during 1985-1997 (multiple interviews).
**SKILL.md captures**: Decision Fingerprint #8 (refused to license), Quote "What ruined Apple was values" in Mental Model #10. 〜 (directional — explicit product licensing story is in the knowledge.json but more lightly in SKILL.md)

### Q15: "What should someone who wants to do great work remember?"
**Documented answer**: "Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish." (Stanford 2005). "About half of what separates successful entrepreneurs from non-successful ones is pure perseverance." (Smithsonian 1995). "Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life." (Stanford 2005)
**SKILL.md captures**: Quote #9 (perseverance), several Stanford quotes. The "Stay Hungry Stay Foolish" could be more explicit. 〜
